The Challenge & Technical Solution

The Problem: Independence at Intersections

While traditional mobility aids (white canes, guide dogs) help with general navigation, they cannot provide:

  • Precise crosswalk location and orientation
  • Real-time heading correction during crossing
  • Traffic signal state recognition
  • Dynamic obstacle detection in the crossing path

The gap: Existing GPS navigation offers street-level guidance but lacks the centimeter-level precision needed for safe crosswalk alignment. Users need to know not just where the crosswalk is, but how to orient their body to cross safely.

Our Technical Approach

Modern smartphone-based systems combine five core technologies:

  • Crosswalk Detection: Camera and computer vision identify crosswalk markings in real-time, calculating orientation even with faded paint or poor lighting
  • User Orientation Estimation: IMU (gyroscope + accelerometer) continuously tracks the user's heading direction
  • Sensor Fusion and Alignment: Combines visual crosswalk data with IMU readings to compute heading error (ŒîŒ∏ = Œ∏_user - Œ∏_crosswalk)
  • Obstacle Detection: Depth estimation detects distance to vehicles, poles, and pedestrians to assess collision risks
  • Navigation Logic: System decides whether to guide rotation, wait, or proceed based on safety-first principles
